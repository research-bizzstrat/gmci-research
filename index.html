Executive Research Abstract

The Global MedTech Compliance Index is an independent empirical study of the structural readiness of In Vitro Diagnostic manufacturers across the Indo-European medical device trade corridor under the shifting In Vitro Diagnostic Regulation (Regulation EU 2024/1860). The study window spans the calendar years 2026 and 2027, capturing the operating posture of the cohort across two consecutive Notified Body application milestones.

The white paper assembles macro-level evidence on three corridor-wide phenomena. First, the velocity of transition file preparation across Class C and Class D portfolios. Second, the magnitude and distribution of capacity deficits at Notified Body level, including scheduling backlogs and documentation acceptance friction. Third, the structural exposure of manufacturers whose continuous market access into the European Economic Area depends on the timely resolution of the May 2026 and subsequent class-specific milestones.

The study produces a single, citable corridor index. The index is published in aggregated form to participating manufacturers, trade attaches, Notified Body capacity coordinators, and policy observers. No individual operational liability, commercial position, or proprietary documentation is ever published or attributed.

The Indo-European corridor concentrates a disproportionate share of global In Vitro Diagnostic supply. The structural posture of this corridor under Regulation EU 2024/1860 is therefore a leading indicator for the resilience of European market access at large.

Methodology

The GMCI methodology rests on four objective milestones drawn from the published transition framework of Regulation EU 2024/1860. The index tracks the position of each participating manufacturer relative to these milestones across Class C and Class D device portfolios under the upcoming transitional deadlines.

  • Class D Notified Body application lodgement against the 2025-05-26 milestone, followed by signed written agreement against the 2025-09-26 milestone.
  • Class C Notified Body application lodgement against the 2026-05-26 milestone, followed by signed written agreement against the 2026-09-26 milestone.
  • Annex II and Annex III technical documentation completeness, including language conversion status for digital portal submission.
  • Class C transition window resolution by the 2028-12-31 final compliance milestone, and Class B and Class A Sterile resolution by the 2029-12-31 milestone.

Data is gathered through three modalities. Direct structured outreach to named regulatory and quality assurance contacts at participating manufacturers, conducted by the Research Operations Desk acting on behalf of Dr. Weber's team. Independent triangulation against publicly available regulatory registries, including but not limited to EUDAMED, CDSCO, and national export records. Qualitative validation interviews with Notified Body capacity coordinators and trade attaches where access permits.

The index reports findings as cohort-level distributions. Individual manufacturer positions are stored privately and used only to verify the integrity of the aggregate. Participating manufacturers receive a confidential single-page positional summary of their own posture relative to the cohort, on request.

Data Integrity and Confidentiality Protocol

The integrity of the GMCI rests on a single non-negotiable discipline. The white paper publishes structural patterns. It does not publish, attribute, leak, or imply the operational liabilities of any individual manufacturer. The GMCI is a purely empirical index and does not offer commercial advisory or consulting services of any kind. The protocol below governs every data point collected through the study.

Anonymisation

Every data point is dissociated from its source at the point of ingestion. All published metrics are macro aggregates. Individual responses are mapped to a research identifier and the bridge file is held under restricted access. No manufacturer name, no individual contact name, no proprietary documentation reference appears in the public index.

Aggregation

The minimum reportable unit is the cohort segment. Metrics are reported as distributions, percentages, frequencies, and ranges. No segment is reported with fewer than seven contributing manufacturers, ensuring that no individual position is reconstructible from the published view.

Secure Processing

Identifiable contributor data is processed inside access-controlled environments with audit logging. Data in transit is encrypted. Data at rest is encrypted. Access to the bridge file connecting research identifiers to source contributors is limited to Dr. Weber and verified research auditors.

No Commercial Disclosure

The GMCI is an independent research entity. The index does not provide legal consultancy, audit services, or certification pathways. The study does not act as a sales channel for any Notified Body, certification provider, or consulting firm. The independence of the index is its foundational asset and is preserved without exception. Commercial queries received by the Research Operations Desk are logged and responded to with a statement of the index's purely empirical scope.

Data Subject Rights

Contributors retain full rights of access, correction, and erasure over their own data. Requests are honoured within thirty days of receipt at the coordination address below. Withdrawal of a contributor's data does not affect the integrity of cohort aggregates already published, but does remove that contributor's responses from all subsequent publications.

Retention

Identifiable data is retained for ninety days from the date of last contributor contact, after which it is anonymised against the research identifier and the bridge file entry is destroyed. The anonymised dataset is retained for the duration of the study and for one calendar year following publication of the final white paper.
